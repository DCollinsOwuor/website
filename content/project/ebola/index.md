Collins was part of the World Health Organization (WHO) deployment of research consultants under the Global Outbreak Alert and Response Network (GOARN) to Monrovia, Liberia during the 2014-15 Ebola virus outbreak in West Africa.

He conducted molecular diagnostics of samples collected from Ebola virus disease (EVD) cases, analysis of data, and reporting of results to the Ministry of Health, Liberia and the WHO. He was part of a team that tested for malaria, which helped to distinguish cases with malaria fever and EVD; this helped prevent quarantine of suspect EVD patients with febrile malaria.

Details on the set up of the deployment site, molecular diagnostics, analysis and data reporting are available from the Ebola publications (select Ebola from the popular topics menu or scroll through the publications list).
