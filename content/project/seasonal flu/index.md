The role of tropical/sub-tropical countries in the global spread of influenza viruses remains unclear due to insufficient spatiotemporal sequence data from these regions.

The movie below shows the global spread of influenza A(H3N2) virus. Globally, viruses spreads from multiple geographical locations to multiple geographical destinations including Africa, suggesting that the global spread of influenza is driven by different geographical regions that also includes Africa; this underscores the need for strengthened surveillance of influenza in understudied tropical and subtropical regions.
