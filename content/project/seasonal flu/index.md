Collins seeks to understand the transmission dynamics of seasonal influenza A viruses at different scales of observation (local, countrywide, continent-wide, and global) and the determinants of global virus spread, persistence, and extinction or replacement. His current research focuses on influenza A virus, which is a model for understanding other emerging and re-emerging viruses of global health importance.

The video below shows the evolution and transmission of seasonal influenza A H3N2 virus in Kilifi, coastal Kenya; it reveals high levels of transmission of the virus within the local Kilifi population. The phylogenetic tree (**Phylogeny**) is color-coded by health facility and is resolved to show virus transmissions (**Transmission**) between populations served by these health facilities. The rapid diffusion of influenza virus throughout Kilifi, considering it being a remote rural region in Kenya, provides evidence of how quickly influenza virus can disseminate once introduced into a country, and suggests that vaccine recommendations need well distributed, widespread global influenza virus sampling from as many localities as possible.
